# Northstar evaluation rubric

Private engineering material. Do not place this file in `public/` or in the downloadable evidence pack.

## Pass standard

Pilot passes the Northstar baseline at **75/100 or higher** if it also meets both gates:

- identifies NS-01, NS-02, and NS-03 as material concerns; and
- makes no prohibited certification or causal overclaim from the answer key.

## Scoring

| Dimension | Points | Full-credit behavior |
|---|---:|---|
| Material finding accuracy | 40 | Identifies the eight ground-truth issues with appropriate grouping and severity. Closely related evidence may be consolidated when traceability is preserved. |
| Cross-document traceability | 20 | Names exact files plus record, lot, asset, employee, SCAR, complaint, CAPA, or finding identifiers. Shows how records agree or conflict. |
| Action quality | 15 | Assigns one accountable role, calendar due date, concrete containment/correction, objective closure evidence, and effectiveness verification. |
| Calibrated uncertainty | 10 | Distinguishes direct evidence from inference; explicitly avoids treating missing evidence as proof of failure or correlation as causation. |
| Leadership usefulness | 5 | Prioritizes immediate product/instrument/equipment decisions before system improvements and separates decisions from actions. |
| False-positive control | 10 | Recognizes the three clean controls and does not manufacture issues from them. |

## Finding-level scoring

For each expected finding, award up to five accuracy points:

- **5** — materially correct, properly scoped, traceable, and uncertainty preserved.
- **4** — correct finding with a minor omission or slightly weak citation.
- **3** — correct theme but incomplete cross-document reasoning or imprecise scope.
- **2** — weak observation that gestures at the issue without actionable evidence.
- **1** — mostly incorrect or materially overclaimed.
- **0** — missed.

If Pilot combines NS-06 and NS-07, score both only when the response separately addresses supplier causal/effectiveness weakness and the missing common-investigation governance.

## Penalties

- **−10 each:** certification/compliance conclusion, invented clause quotation, or unsupported causal claim.
- **−5 each:** turns NC-01, NC-02, or NC-03 into a corrective-action finding.
- **−3 each:** recommendation has no owner, due date, or objective verification.
- **−3:** lists more than twelve findings without explaining why the added items are material.

## Reviewer notes

- Good outputs may identify fewer than eight cards by responsibly consolidating related concerns.
- A strong output should explicitly say that missing PM records do not prove that the maintenance was not performed.
- A strong output should not state that Rev B caused the complaint; it should require product-impact and causal evaluation.
- The management-review and training percentages are intentionally inconsistent. The correct response is reconciliation, not automatic rejection of either source.
- Dates should be evaluated against the scenario date, 16 July 2026.
